Gérard Garouste. Until January 2

Gérard Garouste is one of the main representatives of figurative painting at the time. His images, halfway between surrealism, realism and satire, connect with the work of such important figures as George Grosz, Marc Chagall or Salvador Dalí. The exhibition that opens the Pompidou Center is the first major retrospective that France dedicates to the artist, and brings together 120 masterpieces that include painting, sculpture and installation.

Aqua Mater - Sebastião and Renaissance. Until September 22

This summer, the La Défense district of Paris is hosting a major exhibition by Franco-Brazilian photographer Sebastião Salgado, with a central theme: water. The exhibition is made up of a series of large-format photographs, accompanied by literary quotes that refer to water. The images, as impressive and beautiful as the photographer has accustomed us, capture scenes of glaciers in Patagonia, women fishing in Indonesia or zebras drinking in Namibia, among many others.

Frida Kahlo, beyond appearances. Until March 5

An aesthetic and feminist icon, an image revered by millions of people and an artist of the highest level, the Mexican painter now sees her remembered figure in the exhibition organized by the Fashion Museum of the Villa de Paris in collaboration with the Frida Kahlo Museum of Mexico. The selection of objects is accompanied by a series of films and photographs owned by Frida Kahlo, allowing the viewer to obtain a global image of her life and work.

The golden age of the Portuguese Renaissance. Until October 10

The city of Lisbon was one of the meeting points of the Flemish painters of the 16th century, as the main port of a country of navigators and oriented towards the ocean. This transmission of talent was a milestone in the history of European painting: it was the decision of the Louvre Museum to organize a reasoned selection of works from the period, made by Portuguese artists who knew how to collect and make their own the influences of those from Flanders.
